        ⚠️ What to Check When Buying Used

        These are the known issues and critical inspection points specific to this model. Always get a pre-purchase inspection from an independent mechanic.

          📋 Universal Inspection Checklist

          Applies to any used car purchase:

          • 🔍VIN CheckRun the VIN through a history service to reveal accidents, mileage fraud, or outstanding finance
          • 🛢️Engine OilCheck level and color — milky oil = head gasket failure; dark sludge = poor maintenance
          • 💧CoolantShould be clear/green/pink — rust color means cooling system neglect
          • ⚡OBD ScanPlug in an OBD-II reader — no fault codes means a healthy ECU
          • 🛞Tyres & BrakesUneven wear = alignment issues; listen for brake squeal on test drive
          • 🏎️Test DriveDrive at all speeds; test cold start, acceleration, braking, A/C, and all electrics
          • 🏗️Body Panel GapsUneven panel gaps or mismatched paint hint at accident repairs
          • 📜Service HistoryInsist on full documented service records from authorized dealers
